# Dragon Age - Rules
## Default Timeline(s)
> [!guideline]+ Defaults to Veilguard Era
> ***Unless you request a specific timeline***, I will typically default to one of those of the Veilguard era — either **9:45 - 9:49 Dragon** (*the "Wolf Hunt" phase prior to the events of Veilguard*) or **9:50 - 9:54 Dragon** (*the actual events of Veilguard, including the 6+ months prior from the point when Rook is exiled by their faction leader*).

---

# OC & OC Interactions
## Flexibility Expected
**I *welcome* both multi-protagonist interactions *and* interactions where a muse survives** where they might have otherwise died if they did not fall into the hero role themselves (which is the basis for many of Companion AUs for my DA muses). Even my most stubborn muses are capable of stepping back to let someone else take charge when it's deemed necessary.
## Multiple Heroes
**I am very welcoming to having multiple Wardens (though likely only one would be deemed *the* Hero of Ferelden), additional Hawke siblings, a separate Herald and Inquisitor duo, etc.** 

---
# Worldstates
## Default Worldstates
**I do not have a singular default worldstate.** While I have *preferences* and biases towards certain outcomes, I also enjoy *variety*, and each muse has their own worldstate that they default to, and occasionally alternate ones for specific verses. 
## Custom Worldstates
**I am *also* open to crafting custom worldstates for private verses & AUs** — I am working on putting together a form to assist in crafting custom worldstates, though as I said, it will be limited to those with private verses & AUs, which typically require plotting via discord.
## DNI Worldstate Conditions
> [!rule]+ Dragon Age Worldstate Conditions
> **I will *not* write in worldstates that utilize the following conditions — these are non-negotiable:**
> - Zevran Arainai is killed (*either by Warden or by the Crows*)
> - Sten is not freed and/or does not recover his sword
> - Fenris is given to Denarius
> - Isabela is given to the Qun
> - Dorian Pavus reconciles with his father
## Partners' Worldstates
**Some flexibility is *required*, even when I am only writing a canon muse for your Protagonist OCs**, as many of my canons are canon-divergent, and/or there are some outcomes that I refuse to write at all (such as [[Rulebook/Rules Details/Fandom Specific Rules/! Dragon Age - Rules#Dorian's Personal Quest\|Dorian reconciling with his father]]). I will not compromise *my portrayals* of a canon muse to fit into your expectations for your worldstate.
### Reciprocal Expectations
**I am flexible with my worldstates to allow for adjusting to having other people's heroes step in as the hero in a different part of the timeline** — ie if you write Hawke and wish to interact with any of my protagonist muses, it is very much something that I am both interested in and capable of writing — but this means that I do have certain expectations for worldstate events to be carried on through the series.

|              | Warden (DAO) | Hawke (DA2) | Inquisitor (DAI) | Rook (DATV)   |
| ------------ | ------------ | ----------- | ---------------- | ------------- |
| *Warden*     | W            | W - H       | W - H - I        | W - H - I - R |
| *Hawke*      |              | H           | H - I            | H - I - R     |
| *Inquisitor* |              |             | I                | I - R         |
| *Rook*       |              |             |                  | R             |
So, if I'm writing a Warden, *their choices* would be carried forward throughout the entire series (though I am flexible about *most* of these choices, unless it is something that is outright closed to plotting otherwise). As would be the same for if I am writing Hawke, an Inquisitor, or a Rook — my muse's choices *within their timelines* will have an effect from that point forward.

**I offer the exact same for *other muses' choices* as well**, so if you are writing a Warden in the HoF role, then *their choices* will be the ones carried forward to build the appropriate worldstate from (though again, some flexibility may be required then on *your part* if my muse's choices later on in the series are affected in a way that makes it impossible to stay true to my portrayal — while I *am* flexible about most choices, there are some that are integral to who the muse is and I will not compromise my character at their core).

# Zevran Early Recruitment
**[[Muse Info/! Open Muses/Dragon Age/Zevran Arainai/! Summary - Zevran\|Zevran Arainai]] can be recruited shortly following leaving Lothering and setting camp**, where the wardens suffer the nightmare of the Archdemon. This is based on the Zevran ASAP mod, that is concerned canon for all of my DA muses and verses.

Zevran's ambush is along the Imperial Highway, so their intended destination after breaking camp doesn't matter, as they'd be traveling the same road regardless in order to get there.

---
# Zevran's Romance 
**Zevran may hold feelings for a Warden even if not romanced during the Fifth Blight.** This assumes that they have at least a high approval of friendliness with him, for when he would normally be open to romancing them. 

**This can remain unrequited, or it can be explored with a potential chance of future romance later on in the timeline**, particularly after Zevran has secured his new position as the Eighth Talon of the Antivan Crows.

---
# Dorian's Personal Quest
**Dorian will *never* reconcile with his father when I am writing him, *regardless* of what might be dictated by any Inquisitor's choices.** What his father intended is the magical equivalent of "conversion therapy", which has been and *is still used today* against LGBTQIA+ individuals all around the world, *including* in the United States.

**If your muse tries to convince him to reconcile regardless, it will result in the equivalent "*Dorian Greatly Disapproves*", and will affect roleplayed dynamics accordingly.** Unlike in-game mechanics, however, this *will* likely result in breaking any trust Dorian has built up for your muse to date, and a strong wariness and hesitation of granting forgiveness if or when your muse tries to reconcile with him over it.

---
# Antivan Crows
**The Antivan Crows are *not* inherently the freedom fighters they are portrayed as in Veilguard.** I will *not* ignore their past, as it is what made Zevran who he is, among others, and even the Crows talking amongst themselves will hint about how brutal and traumatic their training often was, but this is not information for outsiders who would not understand. 
## Black Shadow
**Zevran becomes a true bane to the Crows as the Black Shadow** — he is responsible for the deaths of *several* Talons from House Arainai, as well as various Guildmasters and entire cells from a variety of Houses, and likely resulted in some of the minor houses being wiped out completely.
